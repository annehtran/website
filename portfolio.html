Online Retail Dashboard

Analyses £17.5M in retail transactions across 38 countries. Built with a star schema data model, 30+ DAX measures, and 6 interactive pages covering revenue trends, product performance, regional analysis, and customer drivers.

  • Revenue reached £8.2M, driven by higher AOV (+2%), despite a 6.2% drop in orders and flat customer growth.
  • Performance was heavily seasonal, with the UK contributing £6.8M (~85%) and Q4 driving the strongest revenue spike.
  • Top products are PAPER CRAFT LITTLE BIRDIE and MEDIUM CERAMIC TOP dominate both revenue and volume but each have ~50% return rate, while niche categories like Party & Celebration (5.6%) and Lighting (6.8%) remain under‑leveraged.
  • Expand top performers and grow niche categories to lift margins and diversify the product mix.
  • The UK declined 6.6% YoY, dragging results, while France (+30%) and Spain (+45%) emerged as fast‑growing opportunities.
  • Stabilize the UK with win‑back efforts and aggressively scale high‑growth European markets to balance regional risk.
  • Australia is the fastest growing country, up 341.3% vs LY.
  • A -£1.1M volume effect and 54.5% drop in new customers drove the decline, even as pricing added £569K in uplift.
  • Strengthen acquisition and early‑funnel conversion to restore volume and reverse the customer decline.
  • UK revenue hit £6.8M, but orders fell 8.3%, with revenue heavily concentrated among a few high‑value customers.
  • Protect top customers with tailored retention while expanding mid‑tier segments to stabilize long‑term revenue.

Recommendations:

  • Build operational resilience around the November peak. The business depends on a single month for ~18% of annual revenue.
  • Develop a deliberate international growth strategy, investing in Australia as a priority international market.
  • Conduct a quality standards or identify the root defect of the two high-return products immediately.
